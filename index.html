What is cybersecurity and
the importance of it

Cybersecurity as defined, is the practice of protecting systems against attacks or unauthorised access to data. We are increasingly interconnected on the internet, and that is why cyber threats have increased. Cybersecurity is essential to safeguard our personal information, prevent identity theft, and financial crimes . Regardless of whether you are an individual or business, making strong cybersecurity measures is very important for the protection of sensitive data.
Malware, phishing attacks, and ransomware, are just some of the various types of cyber threats. These threats can cause significant financial losses, reputational damage, and even legal consequences. Understanding the importance of cybersecurity and taking proactive steps to protect your digital assets will reduce the risk of falling victim to these threats.

Your data and privacy

It is important to be aware of how your information is being collected, stored, and used by various online services. Personal data is any piece of information that could lead to identifying you, and this includes current details like your name or address as well as other relevant info such as email address, phone number along with all the sites where you navigate. By protecting this information, you can avoid your identity being stolen and financial fraud. We also have strict data privacy regulation such as the GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation), meant to prevent companies from acting irresponsibly with your personal information. To protect your data and privacy, be mindful of what you share online, always use strong and unique passwords, enable two-factor authentication, and check the privacy settings on all platforms.

Browsing on the internet

Browsing the internet is something most of us do daily, but it can expose us to various cyber threats if we're not careful. Having good habits is essential when browsing the internet to protect your personal information and falling victim to cyberattacks. These habits may include using strong, unique passwords for your online accounts and enabling two-factor authentication for added security. Make sure your web browser is always up to date, as updates often include security patches that protect against vulnerabilities. When visiting websites, look for "https://" in the URL to ensure you're on a secure site, especially when entering sensitive information like credit card details. Be cautious of phishing scams, which often come in the form of emails or websites that appear legitimate but are designed to steal your personal information. Don't click on links or download files from unknown sources. Using ad blockers can also help prevent malicious ads from popping up. You can change your browser's privacy settings to limit tracking and data collection, and regularly clear your cookies and cache to maintain your privacy. Finally, when using public Wi-Fi, avoid accessing sensitive information or use a Virtual Private Network (VPN).

Security Habits

Having good security habits can help keep your private data safe online. These are some habits you should have when going on the internet: Create strong, unique passwords for each account you have, enable two-factor authentication for an extra layer of security, always make sure your software is up to date to protect against vulnerabilities, don't click on suspicious links or attachments, install a antivirus software and keep it up to date. Use a VPN if you are connecting to a public Wi-Fi to encrypt your connection, keep checking on your bank statements and accounts regularly for any unusual activity. Keeping up to date on the latest cybersecurity threats and what are the best practices. These simple habits can go a long way in protecting you from cyber threats.

Mobile Devices

Mobile devices have become more and more common in todays' world, however this led to cyberattacks to change their focus more to mobile devices, resulting in the knowledge of mobile security becoming more important. There are many ways you can protect your mobile from cyberthreats such as setting up a strong password or use fingerprint or facial recognition on your mobile device so no one can tampering with it if you leave it unattended, changing your notification setting to decide what is displayed in lock screen (android), and enabling Find My Device or Find My iPhone to be able track, find and even erase your phone. Always enable two-factor authentication on your accounts. Make sure you update your phone and apps regularly to keep everything safe. Download apps from trusted sources like the Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Be cautious when using public Wi-Fi and consider using a VPN for added security. Install a good antivirus app, regularly back up your data, and keep an eye on app permissions to ensure they only access what they need. Look out for phishing scams and never click on suspicious links.
