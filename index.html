Personality Disorders

Personality disorders are a group of mental health conditions that involve long-lasting, disruptive patterns of thinking, behavior, mood and relating to others. Everyone has their own unique personalities, and these personalities allow us to adapt to our environment. People with personality disorder have personality traits that are less adaptive, leading to inflexibility and unhealthy coping. People with personality disorders often have difficulty understanding emotions and coping with stress. They may also act impulsively and have trouble forming healthy relationships.

Symptoms

Symptoms vary depending on the type of personality disorder, but some common signs include strange or unpredictable behavior, suspicion and distrust, difficulty with empathy, trouble with boundaries, and problems maintaining close relationships

Cluster A

Cluster A personality disorders are a group of mental health conditions that involve distrustful, or detached patterns of thinking and behavior. They include paranoid personality disorder, schizoid personality disorder, and schizotypal personality disorder. People with these disorders may have difficulty forming or maintaining close relationships, expressing emotions, or coping with stress.

Cluster B

Cluster B personality disorders are a group of mental health conditions that involve dramatic and erratic behaviors. They include antisocial personality disorder, borderline personality disorder, histrionic personality disorder, and narcissistic personality disorder. People with these disorders may have trouble limiting distorted thoughts, maintaining stable relationships, or controlling impulsive or aggressive behaviors.

Cluster C

Cluster C personality disorders are a group of mental health conditions that involve anxious and fearful patterns of thinking and behavior. People with these disorders may have difficulty forming and maintaining relationships, trusting themselves, or coping with stress. The three types of cluster C personality disorders are avoidant, dependent, and obsessive-compulsive. Each one has its own specific symptoms and treatment options.

Cause

The causes of personality disorders are not fully understood, but research suggests that a combination of genetic, environmental as well as psychological factors may have played a role in the development of personality disorders . Some possible causes of personality disorders include:
- Having a family history of mental disorders
- Experiencing abuse, neglect or trauma in childhood
- Having a sensitive or reactive temperament
- Having difficulties in relating to others or coping with stress

Treatment

The main treatment for personality disorders is psychotherapy, which is a form of talk therapy. Psychotherapy can help people understand their condition, develop coping skills, and improve their quality of life. Medication may also be used to treat the symptoms such as depression, anxiety, or mood swings. Sometimes, when a personality disorder is so severe that you are unable to take care of yourself or if you pose an immediate threat to yourself or others, you are brought in to stay in a hospital until you become stable.

Views

Image by

Personality disorders are often misunderstood and stigmatized by society. People with personality disorders may face discrimination, rejection, isolation, and ridicule from others who do not understand their condition, using their knowledge from movies. Many clinicians avoid diagnosing children with personality disorder because of the stigma around the subject. This is common with many clinicians and is even enforced by some health professionals. Some common negative that people have about personality disorders are:
- They are not real mental illnesses, but rather excuses for bad behavior or lack of character.
- They are caused by poor parenting, trauma, or personal choice, and can be easily changed or cured by willpower or therapy.
- They make people dangerous, violent, manipulative, or antisocial, and they should be avoided or punished.
This prevents people with personality disorders from getting the support and treatment.
